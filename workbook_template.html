Model Methodology — Jamestown Analytics Framework

How we find statistically undervalued players using the Tony Bloom edge-finding approach

Pioneered by: Tony Bloom, Brighton & Hove Albion

The Jamestown Edge — Why This Works

Core insight: In lower leagues (CZ II, Slovak leagues), market values are driven by reputation, agent relationships, and past narratives — NOT by objective statistical output. This creates a systematic and exploitable market inefficiency.

Tony Bloom's Brighton demonstrated that by rigorously quantifying player output and comparing it against what the market charges, a club can consistently acquire talent at a fraction of its true value. The approach requires ignoring reputation, ignoring league prestige bias, and trusting the numbers when they conflict with conventional wisdom.

Our model applies this to the Czech and Slovak football pyramid — where a 25-year-old at Tatran Prešov producing 87th-percentile attacking output but valued at €125,000 represents exactly the kind of edge Brighton exploited in the Championship and League One.

Statistical Quality Score (SQS)

The SQS is a position-weighted composite of Wyscout per-90 metrics, normalised to a 0–100 percentile scale within each position group and recruitment league pool.

SQS = Σ (metric × weight) × league_difficulty_coefficient

Each position group has a bespoke weight matrix prioritising the metrics that most predict success at that position. A striker's SQS weights finishing and box presence heavily; a DM's SQS weights defensive actions and ball progression.

Bloom Index (BI)

The Bloom Index quantifies the gap between a player's statistical output rank and their market price rank. It is the core "edge" metric.

BI = SQS Percentile Rank − Market Value Percentile Rank

Range: −100 to +100. A positive BI means the player's statistical output exceeds their price in the market — they are underpriced. A negative BI means the market overcharges relative to output.

ELITE VALUE ≥30 Priced ≥30 percentile ranks below statistical output. Strong buy signal.
HIGH VALUE ≥20 Significant undervaluation. High priority for scouting visit.
VALUE ≥10 Moderate undervaluation. Worth monitoring and assessing further.
FAIR PRICE 0–9 Priced in line with statistical output. No clear edge.
OVERVALUED <0 Market overcharges relative to output. Avoid unless non-statistical value.

League Difficulty Adjustment

Stats produced in stronger leagues count more. A goal in CZ II is harder to score than in Slovakia II, so we apply difficulty multipliers before computing SQS.

LeagueCoefficientRationale
CZ II×0.82Second tier Czech, professional standard
Slovakia×0.78Slovak top flight, competitive but lower
Slovakia II×0.68Second tier Slovak, lowest difficulty

This means a player in CZ II with a raw SQS of 80 is ranked higher than a Slovak II player with the same raw score — correctly reflecting the harder environment.

XGBoost Model Value

We train an XGBoost regressor to predict market value from age, position, league, minutes played, and SQS components. This gives an independent estimate of what each player should cost.

Value Ratio = Model Value ÷ Market Value

5-fold out-of-fold (OOF) cross-validation ensures the model never predicts on data it trained on — preventing in-sample inflation that would make all players look undervalued. OOF R² = 0.07–0.18, which is typical for football valuation models.

A value ratio >2.0 alongside a positive Bloom Index is the strongest compound signal — the statistical model AND the market rank both agree the player is underpriced.

vs Hradec Gap — Upgrade Quantification

The vs Hradec gap answers the question: "By how much does this candidate exceed our current starters?" We take the two Hradec players with the most minutes at each position (from Impect tracking data), average their quality percentile score, and subtract it from the candidate's SQS rank. A gap of +25 means the candidate is at the 25th percentile better than the current starter — a meaningful, quantified upgrade.

CLEAR UPGRADE
SQS gap ≥ +10 above current starter average. Immediate first-team improvement.
ROTATIONAL / COVER
Near-equivalent quality. Provides competition and depth without clear step up.
DEPTH
Below current starter level. Squad depth only. Rarely recommended under budget constraint.
